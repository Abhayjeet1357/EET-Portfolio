Electrical Estimator Project

Battery Energy Storage Estimate – Inspired by ITI's SaskPower BESS Project

Took inspiration from Island Technical Installations Ltd. And scaled down their "SASKPOWER BESS" project to a building level to understand the various challenges and tasks that takes place in an electrical estimator's job duties.

What This Project Is

A building-scale adaptation of a utility BESS project: I document assumptions, draw a simplified single-line, select representative equipment, and build a transparent materials + labour estimate. This is a training sample (not a bid).

Inspiration: public info on SaskPower’s 20 MW BESS and ITI’s project summary (utility-scale). I’m modeling a smaller building application.

Deliverables

  • Assumptions & scope boundaries
  • Simple SLD (PNG/SVG)
  • Equipment list w/ vendor links & budget prices
  • Excel estimate with contingency/overhead/profit

Why This Is Important

  • Peak shaving: Charge off-peak, discharge during 17:00–20:00 to chop the building’s peak demand.
  • Stability: Fast charge/discharge dampens voltage/frequency blips and smooths renewables.
  • Capex deferral: Lower peaks can defer upstream feeder/panel upgrades.

BESS acts like a grid shock absorber — store when there’s surplus, inject when it’s tight.

Simplified Single-Line Diagram (SLD)

Flow: Utility 13.8 kV → MV Switchgear → Step-up/Down Transformers → PCS E-Houses ↔ Battery Containers → SCADA/Protection.

How It Works

  • Charging: Off-peak; PCS converts AC→DC to store energy in battery racks.
  • Discharging: Peak hours; PCS converts DC→AC through step-up transformer to MV bus.
  • Dispatch: SCADA controls setpoints (kW), SOC limits, alarms, and events.

Protection (High-Level)

  • Relay-coordinated O/C & ground-fault on MV main and feeders.
  • Transformer primary/secondary OCPD, temp monitoring.
  • BMS + DC disconnects/fuses; arc-flash/coordination study for PPE.
