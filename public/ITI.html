System Diagram (Simplified SLD)

Single-line flow (high level): Utility 13.8 kV → MV Switchgear → Step-down/Step-up Transformers → PCS E-Houses (DC↔AC) → Battery Containers (DC) → LV Aux Loads & SCADA/Protection.

Interconnect

MV switchgear receives 13.8 kV from utility. Includes metering, main breaker, protection relays, and outgoing feeder to BESS block.

Conversion

PCS converts battery DC to AC (discharge) and AC to DC (charge). Each PCS block ties to a transformer to match MV.

Controls

SCADA supervises state of charge, temperature, alarms, and dispatch strategy for peak shaving & grid support.

How It Works & Protection

Operation

  • Charging (off-peak): BESS absorbs energy when demand and prices are low to prep for evening peak.
  • Discharging (peak): During 17:00–20:00, BESS supplies ≈1.0 MW, shaving the building peak.
  • Stabilization: Fast response helps smooth micro-fluctuations, complementing traditional generation.

Protection & Safety

  • MV Protection: Relay-coordinated overcurrent & ground fault on incoming and feeder breakers.
  • Transformer Protection: Primary/secondary OCPD, temp sensors, and differential where required.
  • PCS/Battery: BMS with cell/rack monitoring, DC disconnects, fuses, and isolation detection.
  • Arc Flash & Coordination: Time-current curves & PPE category established during studies.
  • SCADA Alarms: Thermal, over/under-voltage, frequency, smoke/fire suppression tie-ins.
