ETAP One-Line: Load Flow & Short-Circuit Analysis

During my Electrical Engineering Technology diploma, I modeled a medium-voltage industrial system in ETAP and ran Load Flow and 3-Phase Short-Circuit studies. Below are the annotated one-line diagrams and a brief write-up of objectives, assumptions, and key takeaways.

Study • Load Flow (LF)

Load Flow Results

The LF study calculates steady-state voltages, real/reactive power flow, and losses under normal operating conditions. My objectives:

  • Validate bus voltage profiles (keep within ±10% of nominal).
  • Check transformer loading & cable current margins.
  • Quantify real power losses and major reactive power sinks/sources.
Study • Short-Circuit (SC)

Short-Circuit Results

The SC study determines fault duties for equipment specification and protection coordination. I focused on:

  • 3-phase bolted fault current at each bus (initial symmetrical RMS).
  • Transformer X/R effects on DC offset and duty ratings.
  • Verifying breaker interrupting ratings against calculated duties.
Overview

ETAP in a nutshell

ETAP (Electrical Transient Analyzer Program) is industry software for modeling, analyzing, and operating power systems. Typical student workflows include: defining a one-line, assigning equipment data (utility source, generators, transformers, cables, and loads), then running study cases (LF, SC, Motor Starting, Harmonics, Protective Device Coordination).

Scope: 39 kV utility → 6 kV MV bus → 600 V LV buses Components: Utility, Generator 12 MW, TXs, Cables, LV Loads Focus: Voltage profile, loading, fault duties
Assumptions & Inputs

Model Assumptions (student project)

  • Balanced three-phase system; power factor per nameplates (e.g., loads 0.97–0.78).
  • Transformer impedances entered as nameplate %Z; positive-sequence R/X as provided.
  • Utility short-circuit strength set by available MVA and X/R; generator subtransient Xd″ per data.
  • Cable positive-sequence R and X per length (ohms/km → ohms @ run length).
Key Takeaways

Results & Learnings

  • Voltage Profile: MV bus voltages held within acceptable range; LV buses showed small drops consistent with cable R/X and transformer taps.
  • Loading: Transformers and cables operated below nameplate limits in the studied case; hottest run identified for future upsizing check.
  • Losses/Vars: Major reactive power flow tracked into LV loads; highlights where PFC (capacitors/VFDs) would have impact.
  • Fault Duties: Highest kA at the first LV bus off each transformer; ensured breaker interrupting ratings exceed calculated duties with margin.
  • Protection Planning: SC results provide the inputs for time-current coordination studies (next step: relay/breaker TCCs).
